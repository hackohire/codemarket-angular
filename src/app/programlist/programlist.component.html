California Housing Finance Agency (CalHFA) CalPLUS Conventional First Mortgage Loan Program with ZIP (HFA Preferred)

Maximum Amount

3% of Loan

Benefits

  • Deferred payment, zero interest rate, long term subordinate mortgage.
  • Guest houses, granny units and in-law quarters are eligible for financing under certain conditions.
  • Can be combined with either CalHFA's MyHome or School programs.
  • Can be combined with MCC.
  • No borrower minimum contribution required
  • Current ownership is allowed. Borrower must provide proof that they have rented at a different location for the past three years to maintain their first-time homebuyer status.
  • Fannie Mae’s Homestyle® Energy Loan option is allowed. Lenders must follow all
  • Fannie Mae and CalHFA’s master servicer guidelines.
  • Program Highlights:
  • *Finance up to 15% of the “as completed” appraised property value. Eligible improvements include:
  • *Energy-related improvements
  • o Weatherization and water efficiency devices, solar panels, wind power devices and
  • geothermal systems
  • * Covers improvements to the home’s resiliency to natural disasters
  • o Storm surge barriers
  • o Foundation retrofitting for earthquakes
  • o Hazardous brush and tree removal in fire zones
  • o Retaining walls to address mud or water flow
  • * Radon remediation
  • * Payoff existing PACE loans up to the maximum allowable LTV

Golden State Finance Authority (GSFA) Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC) Program

Maximum Amount

Annual federal income tax credit of 20% of mortgage interest paid each year.

*An MCC is a federal income tax credit designed to assist persons of low-to-moderate income to better afford individual ownership of housing.
*With an MCC, the qualified homebuyer is eligible to write off a portion of the annual interest paid on the mortgage as a special tax credit, during each year that they occupy the home as their Principal Residence.
*The portion or amount of the tax credit is equal to the mortgage credit rate on the MCC multiplied by the annual interest paid (for example a 20% MCC provides a 20% tax credit). This credit reduces the federal income taxes of the buyer, resulting in an increase in the buyer’s net earnings.
*Increased buyer income results in increased buyer capacity to qualify for the mortgage loan. The MCC has the potential of saving the MCC holder thousands of dollars over the life of the loan.
*Please see the PROGRAM OVERVIEW on the Program Administrator’s website for the Mortgage Credit Certificate Rate used in this Program.

Benefits

  • Provides homebuyers with a federal income tax credit based on a percentage of the mortgage interest paid per year. The tax credit is a dollar-for-dollar reduction against the homebuyer's federal income tax liability
  • In addition to providing a tax credit, an MCC may also help a homebuyer to qualify for the mortgage loan because it may be considered more "qualifying income" during the loan qualifying process.
